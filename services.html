Material Science

Titanium (ASTM-F136)

This is the gold standard for initial piercings. It is an implant-grade alloy that is lightweight and highly biocompatible. It does not contain nickel, which is the leading cause of metal allergies.

14k/18k Solid Gold

We only use solid gold from reputable manufacturers. Our gold is alloyed specifically for body jewelry to ensure it's free of cadmium and lead. We never use gold-plated or gold-filled jewelry for initial procedures.

Niobium

An elemental metal that is 99.9% pure. Great for those with extreme metal sensitivities, Niobium can be anodized into a wide range of colors including a true black.

Procedural FAQ

Can I bring my own jewelry to be pierced with?

Due to health regulations and insurance requirements, we can only pierce with jewelry purchased from our studio. We must be able to verify the mill certificates for the metal and ensure it has been sterilized in our own autoclaves.

How do I know if my piercing is infected?

Some redness and swelling is normal. However, if you experience excessive heat, yellow/green discharge with an odor, or throbbing pain, please contact us immediately for an assessment. We provide free troubleshooting for all our clients.

Why don't you use piercing guns?

Piercing guns utilize blunt force trauma to push a dull stud through the skin, which can damage the tissue. More importantly, piercing guns are made of plastic and cannot be sterilized in an autoclave, posing a significant cross-contamination risk.
